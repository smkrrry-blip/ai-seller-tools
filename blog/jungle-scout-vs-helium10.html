Jungle Scout vs Helium 10 (2026): I Used Both for 6 Months

By Shoichi  ·  Updated April 2026  ·  9 min read

Shoichi

Amazon FBA seller based in Japan · 5+ years on JP, US, EU · I only review tools I personally pay for.

Why I Paid for Both Simultaneously

Most "comparison" articles are written by people who used Tool A for a year, switched to Tool B, and then compare from memory. That's not a fair test. Data quality, UI design, and feature sets change quickly in this industry, and a lot of what you'll find written about Jungle Scout vs Helium 10 is outdated.

In August 2025, I decided to run a proper side-by-side test. I subscribed to Jungle Scout Suite ($69/mo) while keeping my Helium 10 Platinum ($99/mo). For six months — August through January — I used both tools for every research task I ran, recorded my results, and tracked which tool I actually trusted when the data diverged.

Total cost: $1,008 for Jungle Scout over 6 months, on top of my existing Helium 10 fees. Not a casual experiment. Here's what I found.

Head-to-Head: Keyword Research

This is where the tools diverge most clearly, and where Helium 10 wins convincingly.

I ran the same product ASIN through both tools' reverse-ASIN keyword features in October 2025. For a best-selling kitchen spatula set (B09XXXXX — I won't post the actual ASIN to avoid tipping off competitors):

  • Helium 10 Cerebro: 847 keyword variations, with organic rank positions, PPC rank, search volume, and Cerebro IQ Score
  • Jungle Scout Keyword Scout: 312 keyword variations, with search volume and ease scores

That's not a slight edge — Helium 10 returned 2.7x more keyword data. The extra keywords weren't all low-value tail terms either. Cerebro surfaced 14 keywords with 1,000+ monthly searches that Keyword Scout missed entirely. One of them — "silicone spatula set heat resistant" with 4,100 searches/mo — became a primary optimization target in my US listing.

Jungle Scout's keyword data is accurate, and the interface for reviewing it is cleaner. But when you need depth, Helium 10 is in a different league.

Head-to-Head: Product Research

This one is genuinely close. Both Jungle Scout's Product Database and Helium 10's Black Box let you filter by revenue, review count, BSR, price, and category. The underlying data quality appears comparable — in the three cases where I found the same product in both tools, the revenue estimates were within 12% of each other.

The difference is UX. Jungle Scout's Product Database has a cleaner layout and the Opportunity Score (a 1–10 composite rating) is genuinely useful for beginners who aren't sure what filters to set. Black Box is more powerful but requires you to understand what you're filtering for.

For an experienced seller who knows their target product criteria, Black Box is slightly faster to use once you've built your filter template. For someone new to product research, Jungle Scout's guidance is worth the simplicity trade-off.

Head-to-Head: Supplier Research

Jungle Scout wins this category outright. Their Supplier Database pulls from US import records and shows actual verified shipment data — which factories are shipping to which brands, volumes, and frequency. I used it to identify two alternative packaging suppliers when my main supplier raised prices in November 2025. Found a verified Alibaba alternative with 47 prior US shipments to brands I recognized.

Helium 10 doesn't have a comparable supplier research tool. If finding and verifying suppliers is a priority, Jungle Scout has a real feature advantage here.

Head-to-Head: Listing Optimization

Both tools have listing builders that let you input keywords and build your title, bullets, and description. The mechanics are similar:

  • Jungle Scout Listing Builder: drag keywords into your listing and it tracks how many you've used
  • Helium 10 Frankenstein + Scribbles: Frankenstein processes and deduplicates keyword lists, then Scribbles tracks keyword usage in your listing

Helium 10's two-tool approach is more powerful for sellers with large keyword lists, but requires jumping between two interfaces. Jungle Scout's single integrated listing builder is more streamlined for straightforward optimizations.

Practical verdict: both are useful, neither replaces good writing judgment, and both require you to supplement with a generative AI tool (I use ChatGPT) for the actual copywriting work. Call it a tie.

Head-to-Head: Ease of Use

Jungle Scout is significantly easier to learn. The sidebar is clean, the key features are labeled intuitively, and you can get meaningful research done within an hour of signing up.

Helium 10 has 30+ tools and a UI that takes weeks to navigate confidently. In my onboarding sessions with new sellers I mentor, 100% of them find Jungle Scout faster to learn. Helium 10 includes the Freedom Ticket course (included with the subscription) which helps, but it's a real time investment.

If learning curve is a primary concern, Jungle Scout is the obvious choice.

Jungle Scout Wins When...

  • You're in your first 1–2 years of selling and don't want to spend weeks learning a complex platform
  • Supplier research and factory verification are a core part of your sourcing process
  • Budget is a constraint and $69/mo vs $99/mo matters
  • You're primarily doing product validation research rather than deep listing optimization
  • You want cleaner data visualization and the Opportunity Score shortcut for go/no-go decisions

Helium 10 Wins When...

  • You need maximum keyword depth — Cerebro's reverse-ASIN data is unmatched for competitive category analysis
  • You're in a saturated niche where finding non-obvious keywords is a real competitive advantage
  • You manage 15+ ASINs and need the Market Tracker to monitor competitor rank changes over time
  • You have the time and willingness to invest in learning the platform fully
  • You want to track your listing keyword ranks over time and correlate them with listing changes

Clear Winner Recommendation

Choose Jungle Scout if you're a beginner or sourcing-focused seller

The cleaner interface, the Opportunity Score, and the Supplier Database make Jungle Scout the better starting point for sellers in their first two years. You'll learn faster and the data quality is sufficient for early-stage product validation. The $69/mo Suite plan includes everything you need.

Choose Helium 10 if you're an experienced seller optimizing competitive listings

Once you're past the validation stage and managing active listings in competitive categories, Helium 10's keyword depth and tracking tools provide advantages that justify the higher price and steeper learning curve. Cerebro alone is worth the subscription cost if you're running serious keyword research workflows.

Do you need both?

Honestly, for most sellers: no. The only scenario where running both makes sense is if you need Jungle Scout's Supplier Database and Helium 10's keyword depth simultaneously, and your revenue justifies the $168/mo combined spend. After my 6-month experiment, I cancelled Jungle Scout and stayed on Helium 10 — the keyword depth advantage was decisive for the way I work.
